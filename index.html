What is the IT pathway?

Designed for students who wish to engage in an intensive study of computer science and information technologies. All AET IT students will be required to design, develop and conduct multiple research projects while attending the AET.





Who teaches IT?


John Chapin

Courses Teaching 2018-2019: John Chapin teaches AET AP Computer Science A + Data Structures for Sophomores and AET Computer Science Junior Research which will include machine learning, mobile application and web development.


Education: Graduated from Georgetown University in 1987 with a major in Math and received Masters in Business Administration from the University of Virginia in 1996.


Business/Professional: For the last four years I have been teaching Math and Computer Science in Loudoun County Public Schools. I am a career switcher and worked in private industry for 25 years before teaching. I worked in multiple technology roles including serving as a computer systems engineer with Electronic Data systems and co-founding an Internet startup in 1998.


Personal: I have been married for 29 years to my wife Gabriella Kelemen. I have two sons Benjamin and Noah that graduated from Tuscarora High School in 2017 and are attending Babson College in the fall. I have lived in Leesburg for 21 years.


Email: John.Chapin@lcps.org

Peter Randall

Courses Teaching 2018-2019: Peter Randall currently teaches Junior CS Investigations, AP CS, and Integrated Math II.


Education: Mr Randall has a Bachelor's degree in Economics from Western Maryland College and a Master's degree in Computer Science from George Mason University.


Business/Professional: Prior to becoming a teacher, Mr Randall worked as a software developer for several telecomunications companies.


Email: Peter.Randall@lcps.org

Douglas Poland

Courses Teaching 2018-2019: Douglas Poland teaches AET AP Computer Science A, AET Information Technology and Computer Science, and Integrated Math 1.


Education: Mr. Poland received his Bachelor of Science in Mathematics with a Minor in Computer Science at Dickinson College, where he also played football. He earned his Masters Degree in Curriculum and Instruction with a focus of Integrating Technology in the Schools from George Mason University.


Business/Professional: Mr. Poland enters his 15th year teaching in LCPS and his first year at the Academies of Loudoun. He spent his first 14 years at Stone Bridge High School teaching Math and Computer Science. He has coached football and basketball at Loudoun County High School and Stone Bridge High School.
